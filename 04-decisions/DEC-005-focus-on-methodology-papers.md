## Context

Faculty explained three common categories of research papers — review, benchmark, and methodology — and their distinct purposes. See [MEET-002](../meetings/MEET-002-research-areas-and-paper-categories.md) and glossary entries [TERM-001](../glossary/TERM-001-review-paper.md), [TERM-002](../glossary/TERM-002-benchmark-paper.md), [TERM-003](../glossary/TERM-003-methodology-paper.md).

## Decision

Focus primarily on methodology papers going forward, rather than review or benchmark papers.

## Alternatives Considered

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| Review papers | Good for landscape understanding | Already found to require more biology background than currently available (see [DEC-001](DEC-001-computational-ml-first-approach.md)) |
| Benchmark papers | Useful for fair method comparison | Less suited to building an end-to-end reproducible project from scratch |
| Methodology papers (chosen) | Directly proposes a method; aligns with Machine Learning / Data Science interests; supports building a strong end-to-end research project | Requires finding well-documented, reproducible examples (see [DEC-004](DEC-004-reject-paper-001.md)) |

## Rationale

Methodology papers best match the intended skill-building outcome: an end-to-end reproducible research project, not just landscape awareness or comparative analysis.

## Consequences / Outcome

Reinforces the ongoing search for a well-documented methodology paper in miRNA–Disease Association Prediction to replace the rejected [PAPER-001](../01-papers/PAPER-001-mirna-disease-baseline-candidate.md).
