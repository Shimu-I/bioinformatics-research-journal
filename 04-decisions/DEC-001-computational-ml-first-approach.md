# DEC-001 — Approach bioinformatics from a computational/ML perspective rather than biology-mastery-first

## Context

Early exploration of review and foundation papers, intended to build landscape understanding before choosing a topic, surfaced a large amount of biological background knowledge — more than is currently held, given a Computer Science / Data Science specialization.

## Decision

Learn and approach bioinformatics research primarily through a computational and machine learning lens rather than attempting to master biological concepts first.

## Alternatives Considered

| Option | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| Master biological foundations before proceeding | Deeper domain grounding | Slow, misaligned with existing strengths, delays hands-on research |
| Computational/ML-first approach | Plays to existing Data Science background, faster path to reproducible work | Biological intuition builds more slowly, gaps addressed only as needed |

## Rationale

Existing strength is in Data Science and Machine Learning, not biology. Progress is faster and more sustainable by leaning on that strength and picking up biological concepts as needed for the specific problems encountered, rather than front-loading biology study.

## Consequences / Outcome

Directly informed subfield selection in [DEC-003](DEC-003-select-mirna-disease-association-topic.md), which favored a computationally well-defined prediction task.
