# MIST-001 — Considered novelty before selecting a reproducible baseline

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| ID | MIST-001 |
| Date | 2026-W28 |
| Severity | Moderate |

## Context

During the first week, before selecting a base paper, the research process began by thinking about novelty rather than first identifying a reproducible baseline. See [WEEK-2026-W28 — Week 1](../../journal/2026/WEEK-2026-W28.md).

## What Happened

Novelty — what could be improved or contributed — was considered before a baseline paper had been chosen, studied, or reproduced.

## Root Cause

No established workflow yet for how research progresses from topic to contribution; novelty was treated as the starting point instead of the end point.

## Impact

No concrete time was lost on implementation, but the approach was misdirected until corrected by faculty.

## Correction

Faculty corrected the approach directly, explaining the correct order: choose a topic, study several papers, select a reproducible baseline, reproduce the work, understand the methodology deeply, and only then identify limitations and propose novelty. See [MEET-001](../../meetings/MEET-001-baseline-before-novelty-correction.md).

## Lesson Learned

Novelty must come after reproducing and deeply understanding a baseline, not before. This was logged as the first major lesson of the project.
