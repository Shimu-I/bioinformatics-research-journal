# PRIN-002 — Prefer papers with source code, datasets, and reproducible experiments

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| ID | PRIN-002 |
| Date Established | 2026-W29 |
| Status | Active |

## Statement

When selecting a topic or a specific paper to reproduce, prioritize ones that provide public source code, datasets, and reproducible experiments over ones that don't — regardless of how relevant or interesting the topic otherwise is.

## Origin

- [MEET-002 — Research areas, paper selection criteria, paper categories, and PAPER-001 rejection](../../meetings/MEET-002-research-areas-and-paper-categories.md)
- Confirmed by the outcome of [PAPER-001](../../01-papers/PAPER-001-mirna-disease-baseline-candidate.md), rejected for lacking exactly these properties

## Explanation

A paper without accessible code, data, and a documented pipeline cannot be reliably reproduced regardless of how good the underlying method is, and time spent trying is largely wasted relative to finding a well-documented alternative.

## When to Apply

At topic selection and at every paper-selection decision, before committing time to a reproduction attempt.
